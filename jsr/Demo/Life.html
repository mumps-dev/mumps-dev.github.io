John Conway’s Game of Life

Canvass is by squares.      Wrap Around    

Speed:     Slow                       Fast

In this grid, the center cell (the black one) is considered "alive"; the other cells (the white ones) are considered "dead".

When the cell has too many living neighbors, it suffocates and dies.
When the cell does not have enough living neighbors, get gets lonesome and dies.
When a dead cell has the right number of living neighbors, new life is born.

The neighbors of the cell are those immediately adjacent (the green ones).

When a living cell has less than 2 living neighbors, it dies.

When a living cell has more than 3 living neighbors, it dies.

When a dead cell has exactly 3 living neighbors, it springs new life.
